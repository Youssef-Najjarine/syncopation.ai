The Problem

700,000 to 1 million patient falls occur in U.S. hospitals each year, resulting in up to 250,000 injuries and 11,000 deaths. With the aging population expected to double by 2060, fall prevention solutions become even more crucial.

The staggering financial and psychosocial cost of falls affirms the importance of securing fall prevention modalities.

Falls are one of the most documented hospital incidents with up to 1 million falls annually and 2.3 to 7 falls per 1,000 inpatient days. They increase additional hospital stays by 6-12 days and expenses totaling more than $31 billion annually to Medicare.

Many hospitals use alarm systems to prevent falls by alerting staff when patients move without assistance. However, a study of nearly 28,000 patients revealed that alarms don't actually reduce falls or related injuries. While robust staffing is the ideal solution, it remains cost-prohibitive.

Existing solutions fail to deliver.
